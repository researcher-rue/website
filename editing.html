About

I am an early career researcher undertaking a Master of Philosophy (MPhil) in Psychological Research to further develop my skills in clinical research and data analysis. My research interests lie in neuropsychology particularly in examining how early neural abnormalities may indicate vulnerability to developing schizophrenia in adulthood. I am passionate about contributing to early identification and prevention strategies through rigorous, translational research.

Research & Projects

Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia is characterised by subtle premorbid cognitive abnormalities that present years before the onset of psychosis in adulthood. Amenable cognitive functions found to be impaired in both adults with schizophrenia and children at high risk of developing schizophrenia include both early and later neural responses elicited by errors. As certain cognitive abnormalities follow distinct developmental trajectories, this project seeks to characterise the developmental trajectory of early and later neural response abnormalities elicited by errors amongst youth with different risk profiles for schizophrenia
