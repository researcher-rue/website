About

Status: updated

Hello! I am an early career psychology researcher at the Queensland University of Technology, currently undertaking a Master of Philosophy.

My research investigates how neuro electrophysiological abnormalities can be used to identify children at risk of schizophrenia early, prior to significant deterioration in cognitive functioning.

My current Master’s project focuses on mapping the developmental trajectory of a common cognitive abnormality in schizophrenia: error processing. This abnormality is reflected in several event related potential components, including the error related negativity (ERN/Ne) and the later response to errors, known as error positivity (Pe).

My work uses linear mixed effects modelling to examine how amplitude abnormalities in children at risk of schizophrenia evolve across the assumed premorbid phase.

I hope to contribute to evidence based approaches to early identification and prevention in mental health, particularly for severe and enduring mental illness.
